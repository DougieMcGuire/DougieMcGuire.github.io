Great — I went through the full site carefully (Local News, Sports, Obituaries, filters, footer, card layouts, etc.) and here's a detailed breakdown of everything I spotted that could be improved to make TownFeed the go-to feed for everyone in Kossuth County: 🖼️ Content & Images Missing images everywhere — this is the #1 visual problem. Nearly every article card is showing a broken/placeholder icon (a briefcase for business, a globe for sports, a person silhouette for community, a shield for crime). These placeholders are dull and make the feed look unfinished. Fix: Pull Open Graph / meta images automatically from linked articles (most Algona Radio posts have them) Allow editors to upload a featured image manually As a fallback, use a relevant stock photo by topic (e.g., cornfield for agriculture, courthouse for government) 📰 Content Quality & Freshness Single source problem: Every single story — Local News, Sports, Obituaries — comes from Algona Radio only. That's a critical trust and breadth issue. Add sources like the Algona Upper Des Moines newspaper, KIOW 107.3 FM, local school district announcements, county government press releases, the Kossuth County Sheriff's office blotter, and city council agendas. Stale stories in "More Stories": The lower list has articles dated Jun 27, Jun 16, and Apr 3, 2025 — over a year old — mixed in with fresh content. Old stories need to be deprioritized or clearly labeled as archival. Article excerpts cut off inconsistently. Some previews end in mid-sentence with "…" at 10 words, others give 25 words. A consistent 2-sentence preview would be much more useful and clickable. "Farm Safety Day Held Thursday at fairgrounds" has no excerpt/description at all. Several "More Stories" cards are bare headlines with no preview text and no image, making them nearly impossible to engage with. 🗂️ Organization & Navigation Topic filters are great but incomplete. You have Crime, Schools, Government, Weather, Business, Community — but there's no "Agriculture/Farming" tag, which is arguably the most important topic for a county-level Iowa feed. Also no "Events" tag for community happenings. Town filters are incomplete. Algona, Bancroft, Burt, Titonka, Swea City, Wesley, Whittemore, Lu Verne, Lakota, Lone Rock, Ledyard, Fenton are listed — but Corwith, Buffalo Center, and Seneca are missing, which are also Kossuth County communities. No "Breaking" or "Pinned" story designation. There's no way to flag urgent/breaking news (e.g., disaster assistance centers, road closures, weather emergencies) so it rises above everything else. Sports section has a categorization error: "Seton Renovation Project is Underway" appears as the lead sports story, but it's really a school facilities/community story — not sports. Miscategorization undermines trust. No search results feedback. The search bar is present but there's no indication of how it behaves — does it search across all tabs (News + Sports + Obits) or just the active one? This should be clarified. 🎨 Design & UX The dark theme is sharp, but the contrast between the burnt orange/red placeholders and the dark background draws the eye to the absence of images rather than the content. This would be fixed entirely by loading real images. No light mode default. The site seems to default to dark mode for everyone. A light mode default (or auto-detect from OS preference) would serve older/daytime readers better, especially given the demographic. Featured story layout on Local News: The hero card (Farmer's Market) shows only a large burnt-orange placeholder — the biggest card on the page is the most visually broken one. That's the first thing visitors see. "More Stories" section feels like a dump. The lower list switches from rich cards to plain list rows with tiny thumbnails. The visual drop-off signals "less important," making people stop scrolling. Consistent card formatting throughout would help. Obituaries have no imagery or design differentiation. They look identical to a plain text list, despite being a deeply important section for a small-town community. A subtle cross icon (already showing on each entry — ✝) is good, but the cards deserve more dignity — a soft background, maybe a floral or county seal motif. Footer is minimal. It just says "Independent local news · Advertise · Send a tip." There's no About page, no contact info, no social media links, no "submit a community event" form — all of which would build trust and community buy-in. 🔔 Engagement & Retention No push notifications or email newsletter signup. For a feed meant to be the primary source for the county, there's no way to bring readers back. A daily digest email ("Here's what happened in Kossuth County today") would be huge. No comments or community reactions. Even a simple thumbs-up or emoji reaction on stories would make people feel invested. No "Send a Tip" workflow beyond a bare link. The footer says "Send a tip" but community members should be able to submit local events, photos from games/fairs, school announcements, etc. directly in a structured way. No social sharing buttons on article cards. Word of mouth in small towns is everything — one-tap sharing to Facebook especially (huge in rural Iowa) would drive massive organic reach. Live update indicator at the bottom ("Live · Updated 3m ago") is a great touch! But most users will never see it — it's buried at the very bottom of the page. Move it to the top nav bar. ✅ Accuracy & Trust Source attribution is inconsistent. Some stories say "↗ algonaradio.com" (linking out), some have no external link shown, and the "More Stories" rows show "Algona Radio" but without the outbound link icon. Be consistent about showing where each story comes from and making it linkable. Relative timestamps ("1d ago," "10h ago") without absolute dates can confuse for older stories. For anything over 48 hours, show the actual date (e.g., "May 26"). No corrections/update policy visible. For a trusted news feed, showing a brief "We correct errors promptly" statement or a corrections section builds credibility enormously. County scope isn't enforced. "NW Iowa Man Arrested at Emmet County State Park" — Emmet County is a neighboring county, not Kossuth.
